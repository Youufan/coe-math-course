Section 1 of 11

Vectors as Directed Segments

A vector has magnitude and direction. Its starting position can move; its direction and length are what matter.

Core ideaThe directed segment from \(A\) to \(B\) is \(\overrightarrow{AB}\). Its length \(|AB|\) is the magnitude. Lowercase bold letters such as \(\mathbf u,\mathbf v,\mathbf w\) can also denote vectors.
Equal vectors
Same magnitude and same direction, even if drawn in different places.
Negative vector
\(-\mathbf v\) has the same magnitude as \(\mathbf v\), but points in the opposite direction.
TRY Equal or not?
Two arrows have the same length, but one points northeast and one points southwest. Are they equal vectors?
No. Equal vectors need the same magnitude and the same direction.They are negatives of each other if their directions are exactly opposite.
Section 2 of 11

Addition & Scalar Multiplication

Add vectors tip to tail. Scale a vector by stretching it and, if the scalar is negative, reversing it.

Tip to tail: first \(\mathbf u\), then \(\mathbf v\), gives \(\mathbf u+\mathbf v\).
Rules\(\overrightarrow{AB}+\overrightarrow{BC}=\overrightarrow{AC}\). If \(\lambda\gt0\), then \(\lambda\mathbf v\) points in the same direction as \(\mathbf v\) and has magnitude \(\lambda|\mathbf v|\).
TRY Quick vector algebra
If \(\overrightarrow{OA}=\mathbf u\) and \(\overrightarrow{OB}=\mathbf v\), express \(\overrightarrow{AB}\).
Move from \(A\) back to \(O\), then from \(O\) to \(B\).\(\overrightarrow{AB}=\overrightarrow{OB}-\overrightarrow{OA}\).\(\overrightarrow{AB}=\mathbf v-\mathbf u\).
Section 3 of 11

Coordinate Vectors

Coordinates turn arrows into component lists. Add and scale by working component by component.

2 space
\(\mathbf v=(v_1,v_2)=v_1\mathbf i+v_2\mathbf j\)
3 space
\(\mathbf v=(v_1,v_2,v_3)=v_1\mathbf i+v_2\mathbf j+v_3\mathbf k\)
Component rulesIf \(\mathbf u=(u_1,u_2,u_3)\) and \(\mathbf v=(v_1,v_2,v_3)\), then \(\mathbf u+\mathbf v=(u_1+v_1,u_2+v_2,u_3+v_3)\) and \(k\mathbf u=(ku_1,ku_2,ku_3)\).
EXAMPLE Components from points
Find \(\overrightarrow{PQ}\) for \(P=(1,2,3)\) and \(Q=(4,-1,5)\).
Subtract start from end.\(\overrightarrow{PQ}=Q-P=(4-1,-1-2,5-3)\).\(\overrightarrow{PQ}=(3,-3,2)\).
Section 4 of 11

Length, Distance & Unit Vectors

The norm is the vector length. A unit vector keeps only direction.

NormsFor \(\mathbf u=(u_1,u_2)\), \(\|\mathbf u\|=\sqrt{u_1^2+u_2^2}\). For \(\mathbf u=(u_1,u_2,u_3)\), \(\|\mathbf u\|=\sqrt{u_1^2+u_2^2+u_3^2}\).
Unit vector along \(\mathbf u\)
\(\hat{\mathbf u}=\dfrac{1}{\|\mathbf u\|}\mathbf u,\qquad \mathbf u\ne\mathbf0\)
EXAMPLE 1 Norms from the notes
Evaluate the norms of \(\mathbf u=(2,-5)\), \(\mathbf v=(-1,2,2)\), and \(\mathbf w=\left(-\frac1{\sqrt2},0,\frac1{\sqrt2}\right)\).
\(\|\mathbf u\|=\sqrt{2^2+(-5)^2}=\sqrt{29}\).\(\|\mathbf v\|=\sqrt{(-1)^2+2^2+2^2}=3\).\(\|\mathbf w\|=\sqrt{\frac12+0+\frac12}=1\).\(\sqrt{29},\ 3,\ 1\).
TRY Unit vector
Find the unit vector in the direction of \(\mathbf a=(2,-1,2)\).
\(\|\mathbf a\|=\sqrt{2^2+(-1)^2+2^2}=3\).\(\hat{\mathbf a}=\left(\frac23,-\frac13,\frac23\right)\).
Section 5 of 11

Dot Product & Angles

The dot product measures how much two vectors point in the same direction.

Two meanings\(\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=\|\mathbf u\|\,\|\mathbf v\|\cos\theta\), and in coordinates \(\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=u_1v_1+u_2v_2+u_3v_3\).
Positive
Acute angle.
Zero
Perpendicular vectors.
Negative
Obtuse angle.
EXAMPLE 4 Dot product sign
Find \(\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v\) for \(\mathbf u=(2,3,-1)\) and \(\mathbf v=(1,0,5)\).
\(\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=2(1)+3(0)+(-1)(5)\).\(\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=-3\), so the angle is obtuse.
EXAMPLE 5 Angle between vectors
Find the angle between \(\mathbf u=\mathbf i+2\mathbf j-2\mathbf k\) and \(\mathbf v=2\mathbf i+3\mathbf j-6\mathbf k\).
\(\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=1(2)+2(3)+(-2)(-6)=20\).\(\|\mathbf u\|=3\), \(\|\mathbf v\|=7\).\(\cos\theta=\frac{20}{3\cdot7}=\frac{20}{21}\).\(\theta=\cos^{-1}\left(\frac{20}{21}\right)\approx0.310\text{ rad}\).
TRY Orthogonal value
For which \(k\) are \(\mathbf x=(k,k,1)\) and \(\mathbf y=(k,5,6)\) perpendicular?
Perpendicular means dot product \(=0\).\(\mathbf x\cdot\mathbf y=k^2+5k+6=(k+2)(k+3)\).\(k=-2\) or \(k=-3\).
Section 6 of 11

Work Done & Projections

Projection is the shadow of one vector along another. Work is force projected along displacement.

Projection length scales by \(\cos30^\circ\), so it is positive and long.
Projection formula\(\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf v}\mathbf u=\dfrac{\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v}{\|\mathbf v\|^2}\mathbf v\). Work done: \(W=\mathbf F\cdot\mathbf D=\|\mathbf F\|\,\|\mathbf D\|\cos\theta\).
EXAMPLE 7 Work done
If \(\|\mathbf F\|=50\text{ N}\), \(\|\mathbf D\|=30\text{ m}\), and \(\theta=45^\circ\), find the work done.
\(W=\|\mathbf F\|\,\|\mathbf D\|\cos\theta\).\(W=50(30)\cos45^\circ=1500\cdot\frac1{\sqrt2}\).\(W=750\sqrt2\text{ J}\).
EXAMPLE 8 Vector projection
Find \(\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf v}\mathbf u\) for \(\mathbf u=\mathbf i-2\mathbf j+2\mathbf k\), \(\mathbf v=2\mathbf i+3\mathbf j-6\mathbf k\).
\(\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=2-6-12=-16\), and \(\|\mathbf v\|^2=49\).\(\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf v}\mathbf u=-\frac{16}{49}(2\mathbf i+3\mathbf j-6\mathbf k)\).
Section 7 of 11

Cross Product

The cross product produces a vector perpendicular to both input vectors.

Definition\(\mathbf u\times\mathbf v=(\|\mathbf u\|\,\|\mathbf v\|\sin\theta)\hat{\mathbf n}\), where \(\hat{\mathbf n}\) follows the right hand rule.
Coordinate formula
\(\mathbf u\times\mathbf v=(u_2v_3-u_3v_2,\ u_3v_1-u_1v_3,\ u_1v_2-u_2v_1)\)
EXAMPLE 12 Cross product
Find \(\mathbf u\times\mathbf v\) if \(\mathbf u=(1,1,1)\) and \(\mathbf v=(1,0,1)\).
\(\mathbf u\times\mathbf v=(1\cdot1-1\cdot0,\ 1\cdot1-1\cdot1,\ 1\cdot0-1\cdot1)\).\(\mathbf u\times\mathbf v=(1,0,-1)\).
TRY Unit normal
Find a unit vector perpendicular to both \((1,1,1)\) and \((1,0,1)\).
From the previous example, a perpendicular vector is \((1,0,-1)\).Its norm is \(\sqrt2\).\(\hat{\mathbf n}=\frac1{\sqrt2}(1,0,-1)\).
Section 8 of 11

Areas & Scalar Triple Product

Cross products give area. Scalar triple products give volume.

Area
\(\text{Area parallelogram}=\|\mathbf u\times\mathbf v\|\)
\(\text{Area triangle}=\frac12\|\mathbf u\times\mathbf v\|\)
Volume
\(V=|\mathbf u\cdot(\mathbf v\times\mathbf w)|\)
EXAMPLE 14 Triangle area
Find the area of the triangle with vertices \(A=(1,0,0)\), \(B=(0,1,0)\), \(C=(0,0,1)\).
\(\overrightarrow{AB}=(-1,1,0)\), \(\overrightarrow{AC}=(-1,0,1)\).\(\overrightarrow{AB}\times\overrightarrow{AC}=(1,1,1)\).Area \(=\frac12\sqrt{1^2+1^2+1^2}\).\(\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\).
EXAMPLE 16 Volume
Find the volume of the parallelepiped formed by \(\mathbf u=(1,2,3)\), \(\mathbf v=(-1,1,0)\), \(\mathbf w=(1,2,1)\).
\(\mathbf v\times\mathbf w=(1,1,-3)\).\(\mathbf u\cdot(\mathbf v\times\mathbf w)=1+2-9=-6\).Volume is the absolute value: \(6\).
Section 9 of 11

Lines in 3 Space

A line is determined by one point and one direction vector.

Vector equationIf a line passes through position vector \(\mathbf r_0\) and is parallel to \(\mathbf v\), then \(\mathbf r(t)=\mathbf r_0+t\mathbf v,\ t\in\mathbb R\).
Parametric form
\(x=x_0+tv_1,\ y=y_0+tv_2,\ z=z_0+tv_3\)
Cartesian form
\(\dfrac{x-x_0}{v_1}=\dfrac{y-y_0}{v_2}=\dfrac{z-z_0}{v_3}\)
EXAMPLE 17 Line through a point, parallel to a segment
Let \(A=(1,1,0)\), \(B=(0,1,0)\), \(C=(0,0,1)\). Find a vector equation for the line through \(A\) parallel to \(\overrightarrow{BC}\).
Direction: \(\overrightarrow{BC}=C-B=(0,-1,1)\).Point: \(A=(1,1,0)\).\(\mathbf r(t)=(1,1,0)+t(0,-1,1)\), \(t\in\mathbb R\).
EXAMPLE 18 Cartesian to parametric
For \(\ell: x-1=\frac{y+1}{2}=\frac z3\), find vector and parametric equations.
Compare with \(\frac{x-x_0}{v_1}=\frac{y-y_0}{v_2}=\frac{z-z_0}{v_3}\).Point \((1,-1,0)\), direction \((1,2,3)\).Vector: \(\mathbf r=(1,-1,0)+t(1,2,3)\).Parametric: \(x=1+t,\ y=-1+2t,\ z=3t\).
TRY Angle with the \(z\) axis
Find the acute angle between the \(z\) axis and \(x=1-t,\ y=3+\sqrt2\,t,\ z=5+t\).
Direction of the line: \((-1,\sqrt2,1)\). Direction of \(z\) axis: \((0,0,1)\).\(\cos\theta=\frac{|1|}{\sqrt{1+2+1}\cdot1}=\frac12\).\(\theta=\frac{\pi}{3}=60^\circ\).
Section 10 of 11

Planes, Distances & Angles

A plane is determined by a point and a normal vector.

Plane equationFor normal \(\mathbf n=(a,b,c)\) through \(P_0=(x_0,y_0,z_0)\): \(a(x-x_0)+b(y-y_0)+c(z-z_0)=0\). Equivalently, \(ax+by+cz=d\).
Point to line
\(\displaystyle d=\frac{\|\overrightarrow{PS}\times\mathbf v\|}{\|\mathbf v\|}\)
Point to plane
\(\displaystyle d=\frac{|\overrightarrow{PS}\cdot\mathbf n|}{\|\mathbf n\|}\)
EXAMPLE 21 Plane from point and normal
Find the scalar equation for the plane through \(P_0=(1,2,3)\) perpendicular to \(\mathbf n=(4,5,6)\).
Use \(4(x-1)+5(y-2)+6(z-3)=0\).Simplify: \(4x+5y+6z-32=0\).\(4x+5y+6z=32\).
EXAMPLE 22 Plane through three points
Find the plane through \(A=(1,0,0)\), \(B=(0,1,0)\), \(C=(0,0,1)\).
\(\overrightarrow{AB}=(-1,1,0)\), \(\overrightarrow{AC}=(-1,0,1)\).Normal: \(\overrightarrow{AB}\times\overrightarrow{AC}=(1,1,1)\).Through \(A\): \((x-1)+y+z=0\).\(x+y+z=1\).
EXAMPLE 20 Distance from point to line
Find the distance from \(S=(1,3,2)\) to \(\ell:\mathbf r=(0,1,2)+t(1,0,1)\).
Take \(P=(0,1,2)\), \(\mathbf v=(1,0,1)\), \(\overrightarrow{PS}=(1,2,0)\).\(\overrightarrow{PS}\times\mathbf v=(2,-1,-2)\), norm \(3\).\(\|\mathbf v\|=\sqrt2\).Distance \(=\frac{3}{\sqrt2}\).
EXAMPLE 23 Distance from point to plane
Find the distance from \(S=(1,0,-1)\) to \(x-2y+z=1\).
Normal \(\mathbf n=(1,-2,1)\), so \(\|\mathbf n\|=\sqrt6\).Use point \(P=(0,0,1)\) on the plane. Then \(\overrightarrow{PS}=(1,0,-2)\).\(|\overrightarrow{PS}\cdot\mathbf n|=|1+0-2|=1\).Distance \(=\frac1{\sqrt6}\).
TRY Angle between planes
Find the acute angle between \(x-2y+2z=1\) and \(6x-4y+3z=7\).
Normals: \(\mathbf n_1=(1,-2,2)\), \(\mathbf n_2=(6,-4,3)\).\(\mathbf n_1\cdot\mathbf n_2=6+8+6=20\).\(\|\mathbf n_1\|=3\), \(\|\mathbf n_2\|=\sqrt{61}\).\(\theta=\cos^{-1}\left(\frac{20}{3\sqrt{61}}\right)\).

\(\mathbf u\times\mathbf v=(-4,6,-1)\), norm \(\sqrt{53}\).Unit normal: \(\frac1{\sqrt{53}}(-4,6,-1)\).Through \((1,1,0)\): \(-4(x-1)+6(y-1)-z=0\).\(-4x+6y-z=2\).The parallel plane through the origin is \(-4x+6y-z=0\), so distance is \(\frac2{\sqrt{53}}\).

Section 12 of 12

Study Sheet

A one page revision card for vectors.

Big picture
Vectors describe direction and size. Dot product measures alignment, cross product creates a perpendicular direction, and lines or planes are built from points plus directions or normals.
Decision guide
Use componentswhen calculating lengths, sums, and scalar multiples.
Use dot productfor angles, projections, and perpendicular checks.
Use cross productfor area, normals, and vectors perpendicular to two given vectors.
Use line or plane equationsafter identifying the point, direction, and normal.
Formula bank
Length\(\|\mathbf u\|=\sqrt{u_1^2+u_2^2+u_3^2}\)Use to normalise a vector.
Angle\(\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=\|\mathbf u\|\|\mathbf v\|\cos\theta\)Use to find \(\theta\) or test perpendicular vectors.
Projection\(\displaystyle \operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf v}\mathbf u=\frac{\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v}{\mathbf v\cdot\mathbf v}\mathbf v\)Use for the shadow of \(\mathbf u\) on \(\mathbf v\).
Cross product size\(\|\mathbf u\times\mathbf v\|=\|\mathbf u\|\|\mathbf v\|\sin\theta\)Use for parallelogram area.
Line\(\mathbf r=\mathbf r_0+t\mathbf v\)Use a point and a direction vector.
Plane\(\mathbf n\cdot(\mathbf r-\mathbf r_0)=0\)Use a point and a normal vector.
Point to line\(\displaystyle d=\frac{\|\overrightarrow{PS}\times\mathbf v\|}{\|\mathbf v\|}\)Use when the object is a line.
Point to plane\(\displaystyle d=\frac{|\overrightarrow{PS}\cdot\mathbf n|}{\|\mathbf n\|}\)Use when the object is a plane.
Common mistakes
Dot or cross

Dot product gives a scalar. Cross product gives a vector.

Order matters

\(\mathbf u\times\mathbf v=-(\mathbf v\times\mathbf u)\).

Line versus plane

A line needs a direction vector. A plane needs a normal vector.

Distance formulas

Keep the absolute value for point to plane distance.

5 minute self check
1. When are two nonzero vectors perpendicular?
When \(\mathbf u\cdot\mathbf v=0\).
2. What is the area of the parallelogram from \(\mathbf u,\mathbf v\)?
\(\|\mathbf u\times\mathbf v\|\).
3. What vector does a plane equation need?
A normal vector.
4. What does \(\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf v}\mathbf u\) represent?
The vector shadow of \(\mathbf u\) in the direction of \(\mathbf v\).
5. How do you find a plane through three points?
Build two direction vectors, cross them for a normal, then use one point.
